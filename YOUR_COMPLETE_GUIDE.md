## 🎯 What They Need to Understand (3 Key Points)

### Point 1: Month Closing ≠ Removing Cash
- Closing month = Creating accounting record
- Physical cash stays in vault
- Carries forward to next month
- **Example:** K500 in wallet on May 31 → Still K500 on June 1

### Point 2: Two Different Reports
- **Activity Report** = Current month only (what they want)
- **Vault Balance** = Total cash all time (for security)
- Both correct, different purposes

### Point 3: How to Track Monthly Performance
- Dashboard → Activity Report
- Set dates for the month
- Look at "Collections" number
- **That's it!**

## ❓ Common Questions and Answers

### Q: "Why don't vaults start at K0?"
**A:** "Because the physical cash from May is still in the vault. Month closing doesn't remove cash—it just records the end of the period."

### Q: "Where did the K29,800 come from?"
**A:** "K14,590 was already in the vault from May. We collected K15,210 more in June. Total: K29,800."

### Q: "How do we track monthly performance?"
**A:** "Use Activity Report! Set dates Jun 1-30. Look at Collections. That's what the month brought in."

### Q: "Can we make it start at K0?"
**A:** "Only if you physically remove all cash from the vault (deposit to bank, etc.). But then you'd have no operating cash. Standard accounting carries balances forward."

### Q: "Why didn't we know this before?"
**A:** "This is standard accounting—all businesses do it. The confusion is about which report to use. Activity Report for monthly performance, Vault Balance for total cash."

## 🚨 If They Still Don't Understand

### Try the Wallet Analogy:

**You:** "Boss, imagine your personal wallet. On May 31st night, you count it—you have K500. Do you agree?"

**Them:** "Yes."

**You:** "Now it's June 1st morning. The calendar changed. You didn't touch your wallet overnight. How much is in your wallet?"

**Them:** "Still K500."

**You:** "Exactly! The money didn't disappear just because it's a new month. Our vault is the same. The K14,590 that was there on May 31st is still there on June 1st. It's real physical cash."

**Them:** "Oh, I see."

**You:** "Right! And if you earn K300 on June 1st, your wallet now has K500 + K300 = K800 total. But if someone asks 'how much did you EARN in June?' the answer is K300, not K800. That's what Activity Report shows—just what you earned this month, not the total in your wallet."
