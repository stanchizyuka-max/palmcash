# Script to Read to Your Employer (Word-for-Word)

## Use this script when explaining to your employer

---

### Opening (Set the Context):

**"Boss, I understand your confusion about the vault balances. You expected them to start at K0 after closing the month, but you're seeing amounts like K29,800 for KAMWALA SOUTH. Let me explain what's happening and show you how to track what each month brings in."**

---

### Part 1: Explain What Month Closing Actually Does (1 minute)

**"When we clicked 'Close Month' on June 1st, the system created an accounting record. It marked the end of May in the books. But it did NOT physically remove cash from the vaults."**

**"Think of it like this: If you have K500 in your wallet on May 31st at night, and the calendar changes to June 1st in the morning, you still have K500 in your wallet, right? The money doesn't vanish just because it's a new month."**

**"The same thing happened with our vaults. The K14,590 that was in KAMWALA vault on May 31st is still there on June 1st. It's real physical cash from May's operations. Month closing just means we recorded it in the books—it doesn't remove the actual cash."**

---

### Part 2: Explain the Two Different Numbers (1 minute)

**"Now, you're asking: 'How do we track what each month brings in?' That's a great question, and we already have the answer—it's called the Activity Report."**

**"Here's the thing: we have TWO different reports that show TWO different things:"**

**"Number 1 - The Vault Balance pages show total cash in the vault. This includes cash from May plus cash from June. It's useful for security—to know how much physical cash we should have if we count it."**

**"Number 2 - The Activity Report shows only the current month's activity. This is what you want! It shows what June brought in, without including the opening balance from May."**

---

### Part 3: Show Them HOW to Use Activity Report (2 minutes)

**"Let me show you exactly what to do. It's very simple—just three steps:"**

**[Pull up the Activity Report on your computer or show them on their screen]**

**"Step 1: Go to Dashboard, then click Activity Report."**

**"Step 2: Set the date range. For June, put June 1 to June 30. Select your branch if you want to see one branch, or leave it on 'All Branches'."**

**"Step 3: Click Filter. Now look at the number that says 'Collections'."**

**[Point to the Collections number on screen]**

**"See this? Collections: K11,791. THIS is what June brought into our vaults. This is the number you want."**

**"Below it you'll see Disbursements: K14,000. This is what we paid out in June."**

**"So June's net performance is K11,791 collected minus K14,000 disbursed = negative K2,209. We spent more than we collected in these first three days."**

---

### Part 4: Explain the Difference in Numbers (1 minute)

**"Now, you might ask: 'Why does the Activity Report say K11,791 collected, but the KAMWALA vault shows K29,800?' Great question!"**

**"The K29,800 in the vault is:"**
- **K14,590 from May (that was already there)**
- **Plus K15,210 collected in June**
- **Equals K29,800 total**

**"But the Activity Report shows only K11,791 because it's showing collections across ALL branches for June, not just KAMWALA."**

**"The point is: if you want to know 'what this month brought in', always use the Activity Report. If you want to know 'how much total cash is in the vault', use the Vault Balance page."**

---

### Part 5: Action Plan Going Forward (1 minute)

**"Here's what we need to do from now on:"**

**"Every month, at month-end, run the Activity Report for that full month. Record the Collections number, the Disbursements number, and the Collection Rate. That's your monthly performance."**

**"The Vault Balance pages are for security—use them to compare against physical cash counts. But don't use them for tracking monthly performance."**

**"I've created some documents that explain this in detail, and I can print out a simple instruction poster to put at each branch. This way everyone knows: Activity Report for monthly performance, Vault Balance for cash security."**

---

### Closing (Confirm Understanding)

**"Does that make sense? Do you want me to show you the Activity Report right now on the system?"**

---

## If They Ask Specific Questions:

### Q: "Can we make the vaults start at K0 each month?"

**A:** "Only if we physically remove all the cash from the vaults—like depositing it all to the bank. But then we'd have no cash to operate with. In standard accounting, cash balances carry forward from month to month. Every bank, every shop, every business does this. The cash doesn't disappear."

---

### Q: "Why didn't we know this before?"

**A:** "This is how all accounting systems work—it's standard practice. The confusion came because we were looking at the Vault Balance (which shows total cash) when we should have been looking at the Activity Report (which shows monthly performance). It's about using the right report for the right purpose."

---

### Q: "How do other businesses track monthly performance?"

**A:** "Exactly the same way! They have one report that shows total cash on hand (like our Vault Balance), and another report that shows only current period activity (like our Activity Report). Banks do this, supermarkets do this, every business does this. We're doing it correctly—we just need to understand which report to use for which purpose."

---

### Q: "Show me the Activity Report for different months"

**A:** "Sure! [Pull up Activity Report] Let me show you May. [Set dates May 1-31] See? Collections: K[amount]. Now let me show you June. [Set dates June 1-30] Collections: K11,791. Now you can compare: May brought in K[amount], June has brought in K11,791 so far. This is how you track monthly performance."

---

### Q: "Can we get a report that shows opening balance separately?"

**A:** "That's a good idea! Right now the Vault Balance pages show the total, but we could add a summary section that breaks it down: Opening Balance + This Month's Activity = Current Balance. Let me work on that as an improvement. But for now, the Activity Report already gives you what you need for tracking monthly performance."

---

## Summary to Leave Them With:

**"Bottom line: The system is working correctly. Month closing did happen. The vault balances include cash from May plus June—that's correct. To track 'what each month brings in', use the Activity Report with that month's dates. That shows only that month's collections, which is exactly what you're looking for."**

**"I'll print out the simple instruction poster and make sure all branch managers know to use Activity Report for monthly tracking. Any other questions?"**
