## The Simple Explanation

### What Actually Happened:
**The money you see in the vaults IS REAL PHYSICAL CASH that was there at the end of May.**

When you "closed the month" on June 1st:
- ✅ You created an accounting record for reporting purposes
- ✅ You marked the end of May's operations
- ❌ You did NOT remove the physical cash from the vaults

### Think of it Like This:
Imagine you have a cash box with K10,000 in it on May 31st at closing time. When June 1st morning comes:
- The calendar changes to June
- But the K10,000 is **still physically in the cash box**
- You cannot make it disappear just because it's a new month

**Month closing is an ACCOUNTING activity, not a PHYSICAL cash removal.**

## Important Accounting Principles to Explain

### 1. Cash vs. Performance
- **Vault Balance** = How much physical cash you have (includes previous months)
- **Monthly Performance** = What you earned/collected this month (Activity Report)

### 2. Why Opening Balances Matter
If you had K14,590 in the vault on May 31st, and someone steals K5,000 on June 1st, your June transactions might show:
- Collections: K10,000
- Balance should be: K19,590 (14,590 + 10,000)
- Actual balance: K14,590 (someone stole K5,000!)

**Without tracking opening balances, you wouldn't catch the theft!**

### 3. Standard Accounting Practice
Every business in the world carries forward their cash balances:
- Banks don't start at K0 every month
- Shops don't throw away cash at month-end
- Your vault is the same - it's **real money that stays there**
